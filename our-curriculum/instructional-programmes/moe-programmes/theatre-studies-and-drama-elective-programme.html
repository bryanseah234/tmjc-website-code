Theatre Studies and Drama Elective Programme

H2 Theatre Studies & Drama (9726)


The Theatre Studies and Drama Elective Programme explores the human experience through the construction and reception of theatrical events and dramatic texts.  At TMJC TSD, we believe that each student is unique. We aim to embolden our young student-artists to be critical thinkers about the world around them in the practice of their craft through a values-driven education. With the backing of dedicated teachers, supportive parents and encouraging peers, we strive to develop a community of student-artists and learners who are committed and devoted in giving the best to their craft while remaining grounded to their personal beliefs in their work.

OUR AIMS


As a programme, we aim to:
  • develop students’ knowledge and understanding of, and critical thinking about, theatre and drama in a variety of historical and cultural settings
  • stimulate creative exploration of ideas and the expression of these ideas through dramatic forms and theatrical conventions
  • foster enjoyment of theatre and drama and, in so doing, develop the affective, cognitive and aesthetic domains of learning while building personal and collaborative skills

OUR THEATRE STUDIES & DRAMA VALUES


In our programme, we believe in the values of Love, Courage, Faith, Truth & Belief. 

We believe in building a community of learners who love their craft, take ownership of their work and who have the courage to try new and exciting things. Our TSDians (as they affectionately identify themselves at) seek truth in their craft and tell stories that they believe in, through the stage, because they believe in the transformative potential of drama.

Due to the unique nature of this course, students are given the opportunity to develop their skills and confidence in creative mediums they are passionate about, strengthening their courage to express themselves freely and to try again despite of setbacks. Ultimately, TSDians graduate to be responsible and resilient decision-makers who are self-directed in their learning, culturally aware, and active in their contribution to the communities around them. 

OUR LEARNING ENVIRONMENT


At TMJC TSD, we foster a classroom culture that encourages exploration, experimentation and independent learning. Students are encouraged to develop theatrical skills of their choosing and to discuss issues they are passionate about in their performances, whether individually or in a group.

TSD lessons happen within a physical classroom and a studio environment that is stimulating and inviting. Learning takes place in weekly practical workshops focusing on performative skills, complemented by seminar-style discussions and analysis of dramatic texts.

Performance methods covered in workshops and external master-classes range from the canonical practitioners such as Stanislavski, Grotowski, Brecht, Boal, to more contemporary influences like Suzuki method and Viewpoints. Students will also gain knowledge in a wide-range of exciting theatrical traditions (e.g. Commedia Dell’arte, Dance-Theatre, Butoh) and examine how Art movements (e.g. Russian Constructivism, Surrealism, Expressionism) interact with theatrical forms. Beyond the classroom, students are expected to read vigorously to develop a niche area for their Individual Skill practical assessment.

Opportunities for experiential learning and practice will be provided in frequent workshops through the TMJC TSD Masterclass Series. In previous years, the Masterclasses held in our programme have included workshops with theatre companies such as Frantic Assembly (UK), The Finger Players (SG), Nine Years Theatre (SG) as well as professional artists and industry experts in areas such as vocal work and playwriting.
At the same time, the department also organizes multiple learning journeys to watch theatrical performances, enabling the students’ appreciation of the various tools these companies employ. Every year, students are encouraged to see around ten performances by a range of theatre companies to build skills, knowledge and awareness. Students are also encouraged to reflect on theatrical devices used in performance after every learning journey to develop analytical thinking, confidence in articulation, as well as ability to respond critically to creative stimuli.

Finally, TSDians work collaboratively and compassionately. As such, a climate of co-operation and acceptance of ideas that supports and validates our student-artists’ contribution and work in progress is constantly maintained with the aid of supportive alumni and parents who, together with dedicated tutors, provide constant feedback and advice in our students’ creative journeys.

OUR AREAS OF STUDY


Through both analytical study of play texts and genres and the practical exploration of theories about drama, our student-artists will demonstrate the ability to:
 
  • understand and analyse play texts in relation to history, culture and theory
  • produce informed and independent opinions and judgements on dramatic texts, styles and genres and their dramatic effectiveness
  • reflect, analyse, evaluate and record in a cogent and organised way the processes and results of theatrical projects undertaken
  • conceptualise and implement theatrical projects, demonstrate practical skills of theatre and evaluate performance conditions.

THE ASSESSMENT


At the pinnacle of their learning, our student-artist will offer three papers as a candidate for the Singapore–Cambridge GCE A-Level examination:

Paper 1 (40%)
World Theatre and Drama

  Section A
 Forms and Concepts
 (Compulsory)

Candidates will be asked to study a passage taken from an unseen play text and answer a compulsory question and one other question (from a choice of two) from this section. Candidates will answer questions about the way the extract makes use of dramatic forms and concepts to create setting and atmosphere, to indicate character and themes as well as the use of possible theatrical techniques and the actors’ or director’s viewpoints.
 
  Section B
 Greek Theatre and   Drama

Sections B, C and D each specify one theatrical tradition or area. The areas offered for study in these sections will change every few years. 

Currently, TMJC Theatre Studies Students are offering the following texts in 2018/2019:
Elektra Sophocles Section B
Eumenides Aeschylus Section B
Medea Euripides Section B
Long Days' Journey Into Night Eugene O'Neill Section D
Fences August Wilson Section D


 *the playtexts and theatrical areas offered for 2019/2020 will be confirmed at a later date.
  Section C
 Japanese Noh Theatre
 (not offered in TMJC)
  Section D
  American Drama

Paper 2 (15%)
Critical Commentary

The critical commentary is a critical analysis, evaluation and reflection of the creative process leading to the Individual Skills Presentation. It is submitted before the presentation and should be between 2000 and 2500 words written in formal, continuous prose.

Paper 3 (45%)
Practical Assessment
Part 1: Individual Skills Presentation
Each candidate will conceptualise, develop and work on an Individual Presentation showcasing an area of specialisation and related skills in theatre.

Candidates’ choice of an area or skill for the Individual Presentation must fall within one of the three broad categories below:
(i) Repertoire: acting, stage movement†
(ii) Devised drama: dramatic sequence, puppetry, stage movement†
(iii) Design: set, make-up and costume, lighting, sound, masks

Part 2: Group Presentation

Candidates in groups of 4 to 6 will present either an adaptation and performance of a published play text, or a performance of an original work/theatrical presentation of their own invention.
